Who We Are

Girls Who Code at Georgia Tech is a club of female and nonbinary students passionate about empowering and creating community for students interested in STEM, especially those who are traditionally underrepresented. Our goal is to provide elementary, middle, and high school students with role models and support in their CS-education journeys. We’re also dedicated to providing resources to our members in their professional journeys.

What We Do

We develop curriculums for and teach weekly/biweekly coding lessons at schools in the Atlanta Metropolitan Area. For Girls Who Code members, we create social and professional development events to prepare them for the professional world.

Our Schools

As part of our mission to help younger students explore computer science, we partner with elementary, middle, and high schools every year. Below are the schools that we are working with this year.

     
    Sutton Middle School

    Sutton Middle School is an Atlanta public school serving 6th-8th graders. The curriculum taught in hybrid style is an introduction to Python, which covers variables, data types, boolean expressions, math operators, comparison operators, conditionals, loops, lists, and nested lists. Some weeks cover Makecode Arcade and Scratch. Our chapter has established a club there called Coders of the Future. In our partnership with Best Buddies, Sutton donated a game called Cart Smash!

    North Atlanta High School

    North Atlanta High School is a public high school in Atlanta with students from grades 9-12. The curriculum covers introductory concepts in Python such as data types, boolean expressions, math operators, comparison operators, conditionals, loops, lists, and nested lists. We volunteer for North Atlanta High School’s Girls Who Code club that meets before school. Despite our meetings being virtual, the students were engaged and actively participating in answering questions we had prepared for them.

    Midtown High School

    Midtown High School is a public school in Atlanta, which has students from the 9th to the 12th grade. This semester, during their engagement with GWC at GT, the students were taught in-person about the introductory topics of Python, including data types, boolean expressions, conditionals, and loops. Some students at Midtown were advanced and were ready to tackle more challenge problems in these topics as well. Midtown has a Girls Who Code chapter established in their school, and GWC at GT is delighted to be partnering with them. On the Best Buddies front, Midtown donated their version of the Flappy Bird game.

    Centennial Academy

    Centennial Academy is a charter school in Atlanta serving students in K-8. Our chapter teaches two cohorts in-person: students in 3rd through 5th grade, and students in 6th through 8th grade. The curriculum is taught in Python and covers introductory topics like data types, lists, arithmetic operators, and loops. We are partnered with their club Heroes Who Code.

    Best Buddies

    For the 2021-22 year, GT Girls Who Code also partnered with Mill Creek High School’s Best Buddies program. What Best Buddies does is encourage local high and middle schoolers to code games and donate them to Mill Creek. Mill Creek High School then distributes these games to its students with autism. The goal of this partnership is to enhance the lives of people with intellectual disabilities by providing opportunities for interaction and connection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can join Girls Who Code at Georgia Tech?

Graduate and undergraduate students of all majors are welcome!

What is the difference between General Members and Facilitators?

General Members have access to all our corporate networking events, job/internship and other career opportunity postings, and club-wide social events.

Facilitators are members who are selected through an application process to teach at our partner schools. They are also a part of one of four committees. Facilitators have access to all the resources that our General Members do.

What are committees and what do they do?

We have four main committees that help run our regular operations:

Education: This team shapes the material that we teach our students! Team members find interactive curricula and create engaging lessons and projects for the students to learn coding.

Networking: This team reaches out to potential sponsors, builds partnerships, and creates networking and professional development opportunities for our members.

Social: This team runs the social media accounts for Girls Who Code @ GT and manages our alumni network! They work to promote the club around campus and design our graphics & swag.

Event Planning: This team plans social events for facilitators and general members. This helps to foster a welcoming environment within the club.

What is the time committment?

General Members can commit as much or as little time as they would like.

Facilitators must meet with and complete work for their respective committee (about 1-3 hours per week). Facilitators also need to teach a number of times per semester, but they can choose when they teach.

Is your organization known for any particular events, programs, activities, or other elements?

GWC hosts social and professional development events for all members. For facilitators, Girls Who Code at GT additionally connects them with elementary, middle, and high school students, who they teach and mentor. There are also facilitator-exclusive events held throughout the year.

Does your organization collect dues?

For facilitators, no. Starting Fall 2022, Girls Who Code general members will pay $5 for the year, which gives them access to our various social and networking events.

Will your organization host meetings/events in-person, virtually, or hybrid for academic year 2022-2023?

Meetings/events will be hosted in all formats.
